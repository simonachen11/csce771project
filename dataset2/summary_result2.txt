(WIS).The number of reported incidents of hate crimes in South Carolina dropped slightly from 2020 to 2021, according to the FBI, which released new data Monday that shows reported incidents also decreased nationwide in that time.Last year, law enforcement agencies reported 106 incidents of hate crimes around South Carolina, a small decrease from the 110 reported in 2020. While the number of reported incidents of hate crimes in South Carolina slightly dipped from 2020 to 2021, they are nearly double the 57 reported just a few years ago in 2019.The FBI Uniform Crime Reporting Program classifies hate crimes as criminal offenses motivated by the offender  bias against a race, religion, disability, sexual orientation, ethnicity, gender, or gender identity
